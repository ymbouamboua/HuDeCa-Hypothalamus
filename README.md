# A Spatially Resolved Single-Cell Atlas of the Developing Human Fetal Hypothalamus

The hypothalamus orchestrates essential neuroendocrine functions including metabolism, reproduction, and stress responses, yet its early human developmental programs remain poorly defined. Here, we generate a single-cell transcriptomic atlas of the developing human fetal hypothalamus spanning post-conception weeks 7–12. Following rigorous quality control and reciprocal PCA–based integration, we reconstructed the cellular landscape of early hypothalamic development across multiple fetal samples. Our analysis identifies diverse progenitor populations, including radial glia and intermediate progenitors, and resolves their differentiation into distinct neuronal and glial lineages. Within the neuronal compartment, we uncover transcriptionally defined populations corresponding to emerging hypothalamic nuclei and neuroendocrine cell types, including POMC, AGRP, NPY, and KISS1 neurons associated with the arcuate nucleus. Additional domain-specific transcriptional programs marked by SIM1, TBX3, PITX2, OTX2, and NKX2-1 reveal early regional patterning of hypothalamic circuits. Together, this atlas provides a comprehensive resource for understanding the cellular and molecular programs that establish human hypothalamic neuroendocrine systems during early development.
